Transport for London (TfL) Fare Overview

London’s transport network uses a zonal fare system covering Zones 1 to 9. 
Zone 1 covers central London, with higher-numbered zones extending towards 
outer London. Most journeys within central London take place in Zones 1–2.

Payment Methods:
Passengers can pay using contactless bank cards/devices or an Oyster card 
with Pay As You Go. Fares are calculated based on the zones travelled, the 
transport mode used, and the time of travel. Always touch in at the start of 
a journey and touch out at the end when required to ensure the correct fare 
is charged. 

Peak and Off-Peak Travel:
Peak fares generally apply on weekdays during the busiest commuting periods 
(typically Monday to Friday, 06:30–09:30 and 16:00–19:00). Off-peak fares 
apply outside these periods, including weekends and public holidays, and are 
usually cheaper.

Daily and Weekly Fare Capping:
Contactless and Oyster Pay As You Go users automatically benefit from fare 
capping. Once the total cost of journeys reaches the daily or weekly cap for 
the zones travelled, additional eligible journeys are not charged. Caps depend 
on the zones and services used. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Buses and Trams:
Bus and tram journeys use a flat Pay As You Go fare rather than the zone 
system. The Hopper fare allows unlimited bus and tram transfers within one 
hour when using the same payment method. 

Children and Discounted Travel:
Children under 11 can travel free on many TfL services when meeting the 
eligibility requirements, including free travel on buses and trams. Children 
aged 5–10 and older children may require appropriate Zip Oyster photocards 
for certain services or discounted travel. 

Fare Checking:
Passengers can use TfL’s fare finder tools to check the cost of journeys 
between stations, as fares may vary depending on route, time, and service 
used. 

Step free access:
 94 Tube stations (more than a third), more than 60 London Overground stations and all 41 Elizabeth line stations have step-free access. All DLR stations and tram stops are step-free, and all our buses and the majority of our bus stops are wheelchair-accessible.
Ticket and Payment Types:
Contactless: Pay using bank cards or mobile devices (Apple Pay/Google Pay).
Oyster: Reusable smartcard for Pay As You Go or Season Tickets.
Hopper Fare: Unlimited bus and tram transfers within one hour using the same payment method.
Caps: Daily and Weekly fare caps apply automatically when using Contactless or Oyster.
Travelcards: Paper or electronic tickets covering multiple zones for unlimited travel over a set period.
Discounts: Railcard holders can add discounts to their Oyster card at station ticket machines.


Airport Travel Guide:

1. Heathrow Airport (LHR)
 Lines: Heathrow Express (Paddington), Elizabeth line, Piccadilly line (Underground).
Journey Time: Heathrow Express (~15 mins), Elizabeth line (~30-45 mins), Piccadilly line (~50 mins).
Fares: Heathrow Express (~£25), Elizabeth line (~£13), Piccadilly line (~£5.80). 
Notes: Oyster/Contactless is accepted on all rail services to Heathrow. Night Tube service is available on the Piccadilly line on Friday and Saturday nights (excluding Terminal 4).

2. Gatwick Airport (LGW)
 Lines: Gatwick Express (Victoria), Southern and Thameslink (Victoria, London Bridge, St Pancras).
Journey Time: Gatwick Express (~30 mins), Thameslink/Southern (~30-60 mins depending on origin).
Fares: Gatwick Express (~£15.70 - £24+), Thameslink/Southern (varies).
Notes: Oyster/Contactless is accepted on all train services between London and Gatwick.

3. Stansted Airport (STN)
 Lines: Stansted Express (Liverpool Street).
Journey Time: ~45-47 mins.
 Fares: From ~£19.50.
 Notes: Contactless and Oyster are NOT accepted on the Stansted Express. You must purchase a separate rail ticket.

4. Luton Airport (LTN)
Lines: Thameslink or East Midlands Railway (from St. Pancras International).
Journey Time: ~22-45 mins (plus shuttle transfer from Luton Airport Parkway).
 Fares: Varies by service and advance booking.
 Notes: Requires a shuttle connection from the station to the terminal.

5. London City Airport (LCY)
Lines: Docklands Light Railway (DLR).
Journey Time: ~20 mins (to Bank).
Fares: Standard DLR/Tube fares (Peak ~£3.70 / Off-peak ~£3).
Notes: Fully integrated into the TfL network; Contactless and Oyster are accepted.

General Airport Travel Tips:
Payment: Always check if your specific rail service accepts Contactless/Oyster (e.g., Stansted Express does not).
Planning: Use the TfL Journey Planner for live status updates and the most efficient routes.
Night Services: While some lines have limited hours, National Express coaches provide 24/7 links from many airports to central London.

Night Services Guide:

1. Night Tube
- Operating Nights: Friday and Saturday nights.
- Times: Services run continuously from around midnight to 05:30 am.
- Lines and Frequencies:
  * Victoria Line: Every 10 minutes (full route).
  * Jubilee Line: Every 10 minutes (full route).
  * Central Line: Every 10 minutes (White City to Leytonstone); every 20 minutes on outer branches.
  * Northern Line: Every 8–15 minutes (Charing Cross branch only).
  * Piccadilly Line: Every 10 minutes (Cockfosters to Heathrow Terminal 5).

2. Night Overground
- Operating Nights: Friday and Saturday nights.
- Route: Windrush line between Highbury & Islington and New Cross Gate.
- Frequency: Every 15 minutes.

3. Night Buses
- Operating Nights: Every night of the week.
- Coverage: An extensive network of routes prefixed with "N" (e.g., N29, N73) covers the city when the Tube is not running.
- Frequency: Typically every 15–30 minutes.

4. General Information
- Fares: Night Tube travel is charged at off-peak rates.
- Payment: Contactless, Oyster, and Travelcards are accepted (Cash is not accepted).
- Safety: Night Tube stations remain staffed, and British Transport Police patrol the network.
- Engineering Works: Maintenance usually occurs Sunday through Thursday nights; always check the TfL live status page for real-time disruptions.

Frequently Asked Questions:
- Can I use Apple Pay? Yes, you can use any contactless-enabled device.
- What if I forget to touch out? You may be charged a maximum fare. Contact TfL support for corrections.
- Can two people use the same Oyster? No, each passenger needs their own payment method.
- Do children travel free? Children under 11 travel free on buses and trams when accompanied by an adult.
- What is the Hopper fare? It allows unlimited bus/tram transfers within 60 minutes for free after the first fare.
